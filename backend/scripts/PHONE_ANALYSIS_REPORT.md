## 🔍 KEY FINDINGS & OBSERVATIONS

### High-Confidence Statewide Infrastructure (Universal Numbers):
1. **911** - Emergency (100%, 58/58)
2. **211** - Information/Referral (100%, 58/58)
3. **988** - Suicide/Crisis (98.3%, 57/58)

### Strong Regional Standards:
- **Poison Control (800-222-1222)**: 87.9% coverage - NOT in all counties (major gap analysis needed)
- **CalVCB (800-777-9229)**: 77.6% coverage - Crime victim services
- **Consumer Affairs (800-952-5210)**: 77.6% coverage - Regulatory assistance
- **Road Info (511)**: 77.6% coverage - Transportation

### Regional Clustering Pattern:
- **Bay Area**: Distinctive 211 and AQMD services (4 counties)
- **Central Valley**: Agricultural and air quality services (8 counties)
- **Northern California**: NorCal 211 partnership (4 counties)
- **Southern California**: South Coast AQMD region (4 counties)
- **Eastern Sierra**: Mountain region services (3 counties)

### Data Quality Observations:
- **211 Naming Inconsistency**: 10+ different service name variations for phone 211 across counties
- **Consumer Affairs Naming**: 2 variations ("CA Dept of Consumer Affairs" vs "CA Dept. of Consumer Affairs")
- **Crisis Lines**: Minor naming differences (dashes, special characters)

### Service Distribution Gaps:
- **Poison Control GAP**: Missing from ~13 counties (22.4%)
  - Northern counties: Marin, Sonoma, Butte, Colusa, Glenn, Humboldt, Lassen, Lake
  - Coastal: Orange, Placer, Siskiyou, Trinity
  
- **988 Crisis Line GAP**: Missing from 1 county (San Francisco - 1.7%)
